# Series 11: Learned Polynomial + Per-Layer Adaptive + Selective Un-Equalization

**Files:** `cum/cum_11v1.py`, `cum/cum_11v2.py`, `cum/cum_11v3.py`
**Status:** 11a TESTED (FAILED), 11b TESTED (11v3 promising)

## Hypothesis

### 11v1: Learned Polynomial Coefficients
The standard NS polynomial (3.4445, -4.7750, 2.0315) has an UNSTABLE fixed point at σ≈0.868
(|p'|≈1.58 > 1). SVs oscillate around it. A "stable-0.88" polynomial (2.0, -1.940, 0.836)
has p(0.88)≈0.88 and p'(0.88)≈0 — super-stable, no oscillation.

If stable works: oscillation was a bug, blending was a patch.
If stable fails: oscillation provides beneficial diversity/regularization.

### 11v2: Per-Layer Adaptive Combined
Different layers lose different amounts of curvature to NS. Measure "change ratio"
(how much NS changes the gradient), scale blend weight proportionally. Adapts the
AMOUNT of simple averaging (preserves statistical cancellation), unlike failed
adaptive_residual which weighted the CORRECTIONS.

### 11v3: Selective Un-Equalization
After combined mode, restore 5% of curvature via row-wise scaling. Track gradient
row-norm² EMA. Scale: (1-α) + α·clamp(row_curv/mean_curv, 0.5, 2.0).
Different from failed v11: 5% not 100%, row-wise not element-wise, on combined base.

## Architecture
```
11v1: grad → momentum → custom NS(a,b,c) [→ iterate blend → temporal blend] → update
11v2: grad → momentum → NS multi-res → measure change → adapt blend → combined → update
11v3: grad → row-norm EMA → momentum → [combined mode] → row-scale (5%) → update
```

## Key Formulas

### 11v1 Stable-0.88 polynomial
p(σ) = 2.0σ - 1.940σ³ + 0.836σ⁵
p(0.88) = 0.879 ≈ 0.88, p'(0.88) ≈ 0.0

### 11v2 Adaptive blend
change_ratio = ||NS(u/||u||) - u/||u|||| / ||u/||u||||
adapted_blend = min(base_blend × (1 + scale × change_ratio), 0.5)

### 11v3 Un-equalization
row_curv_t = β·row_curv_{t-1} + (1-β)·||g_row||²
scale_row = (1-α) + α·clamp(row_curv / mean_curv, 0.5, 2.0)

## Analysis (11a)

### Combined mode replicates
8v1 combined: 1.5062 (-0.016 vs Muon). Previous run: 1.5008 (-0.019). Delta between runs: 0.005, within ±0.006 noise band. **Combined is confirmed real across 2 runs.**

### Stable polynomial catastrophically fails
11v1 stable basic: +0.045 vs Muon. 11v1 stable combined: +0.032 vs Muon. Both are 4-5x above noise floor — this is NOT noise.

The trajectory reveals the mechanism: at step 500, stable basic is 0.28 BEHIND Muon (2.075 vs 1.793). The gap narrows over training but never closes. The stable polynomial converges too slowly from step 1.

### Why it fails: aggressive equalization requires large coefficients
The standard NS polynomial has large coefficients (3.4445, -4.7750, 2.0315). These AGGRESSIVELY pull small SVs upward: σ=0.1 → 0.34 in one step. After 5 steps, all SVs are crammed into a narrow band around 0.88.

The stable polynomial has small coefficients (2.0, -1.940, 0.836). σ=0.1 → 0.20 in one step. After 5 steps, small SVs haven't been pulled up enough — significant SV spread remains. The equalization is incomplete.

**The large coefficients needed for aggressive equalization ARE what cause the fixed point instability (|p'(0.868)|=1.58 > 1).** You can't decouple them with a degree-5 polynomial in 5 steps. The standard polynomial pays the oscillation price; blending recovers the cost.

### Blending is not a "patch" — it's Phase 2
The winning recipe is a two-phase process:
1. **Phase 1** (standard NS): Aggressively equalize SVs, accepting oscillation as side effect
2. **Phase 2** (blending): Cancel the oscillation while keeping the equalization

The stable polynomial tried to skip Phase 1. It got no oscillation but also no equalization. Blending on top (stable combined, -0.013 vs stable basic) helps slightly (probably momentum-smoothing) but can't fix the fundamental deficit.

### Revised understanding of oscillation
**Learning 23 needs revision.** The oscillation isn't "the feature" per se — it's the UNAVOIDABLE SIDE EFFECT of aggressive equalization. The feature is the equalization itself. Blending is the mechanism to extract the equalization benefit while canceling the oscillation cost.

## Verdict (11a)
**11v1 FAILED.** Stable-0.88 polynomial catastrophically worse (+0.032-0.045 vs Muon). Aggressive SV equalization requires large polynomial coefficients, which inherently cause oscillation. The oscillation is the price of equalization, and blending is the mechanism to pay it cleanly. Combined mode replicated at -0.016 vs Muon (2nd run).

## Analysis (11b)

### Combined replicates for 3rd time
8v1 combined: 1.5091 (-0.012 vs Muon). Three runs: -0.019, -0.016, -0.012. Mean: **-0.016 ± 0.004**. Combined is solidly confirmed.

### 11v3 un-equalization edges combined
11v3: 1.5077 (-0.0014 vs combined). Absolute delta is within noise, but trajectory pattern is suggestive:
- Steps 250-500: 11v3 BEHIND combined (row-norm EMA warming up, curvature proxy is noisy)
- Step 1000: 11v3 catches up (-0.003 vs combined)
- Steps 1500+: 11v3 pulls ahead. Best checkpoint: 1.5021 at step 1750 vs combined's 1.5037

The "behind early, ahead late" pattern is consistent with the mechanism: the row-norm² EMA needs training steps to converge. Once it has a reliable curvature estimate, the 5% reweighting adds real signal. ONE RUN — needs replication.

### 11v2 adaptive fails
11v2: +0.0021 vs combined. Per-layer blend adaptation doesn't help. Extends learnings 36-37: even adapting the AMOUNT of simple averaging (not weighting logic) doesn't beat uniform weights. Combined's fixed blend is already near-optimal across layers.

### Three-axis improvement stack (if 11v3 replicates)
1. **Within-step iterate blend** (NS₂ + NS₅ → oscillation cancellation)
2. **Across-step temporal blend** (EMA → temporal oscillation cancellation)
3. **Post-blend curvature recovery** (5% row-wise un-equalization → information recovery)

Each layer addresses a different problem: (1) within-step SV scatter, (2) across-step SV scatter, (3) information loss from equalization itself. If these are truly orthogonal, the stack should be approximately additive.

## Verdict (11b)
**11v2 FAILED.** Per-layer adaptive blend (+0.002 vs combined) doesn't help. Smart adaptation of uniform blend amounts is unnecessary.

**11v3 PROMISING.** Un-equalization edges combined (-0.0014) with suggestive trajectory pattern (behind early → ahead late). If real, this is a THIRD axis of improvement on top of double oscillation cancellation. Needs replication and α sweep.
