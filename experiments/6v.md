## Analysis

### Why everything targeting SVs=1.0 fails
Polar Express, Dion (full rank), Halley, and QDWH all compute better approximations to the exact polar factor (SVs = 1.0). They're solving the WRONG problem. NS's oscillating polynomial gives SVs ≈ 0.877, and that sub-unity contraction provides spectral norm regularization that exact polar doesn't.

### Why MARS fails catastrophically
MARS applies NS to the gradient difference (g_t - g_{t-1}). But gradient differences between consecutive steps are tiny (momentum β=0.95). NS applied to a near-zero matrix produces essentially a random orthogonal matrix. This random correction dominates the actual gradient signal.

### Why warm-starting NS fails
Starting NS from the previous step's polar factor (already near-orthogonal, SVs ≈ 0.877) means NS barely modifies it — the iteration is already near its fixed point. Result: the optimizer keeps repeating the previous step's direction regardless of the current gradient.

### Why shrinkage blend is closest
6v8 blend mode combines Donoho-Gavish denoising with NS₅ polynomial output. This is the only tested approach that both equalizes (via NS₅ component) AND does something principled about noise. Still loses to Muon because the blend ratio isn't tuned.

### Speed observations
SVD-based methods (6v2, 6v4, 6v8, 6v9) are ~140s vs Muon's 30s on H100. SVD isn't parallelizable like matmuls. Matrix-iteration methods (6v1 at 20s, 6v6 at 26s) are competitive on speed but not quality.

## Verdict
**ALL FAILED.** Confirms that NS's specific oscillating polynomial at ~0.877 is extraordinarily hard to beat. The ONLY approach that has ever beaten Muon remains the v5/5v6 NS intermediate blend.
