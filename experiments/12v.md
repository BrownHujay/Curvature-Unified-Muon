# Series 12: Bifurcation Sweep, TD(lambda), Adaptive Scheduling

**Files:** `cum/cum_12v1.py`, `cum/cum_12v2.py`, `cum/cum_12v3.py`
**Status:** TESTED

## Hypothesis

### 12v1: Bifurcation Diagram Sweep
The standard NS polynomial has |p'(σ*)|≈1.58 — just past the period-doubling bifurcation.
Deep research predicts an inverted-U performance curve peaking in the period-2 regime
(|p'| ∈ [1.2, 1.8]). The standard polynomial might not be optimal.

Sweep p'(σ*) from -1.0 to -2.8 while fixing the fixed point and c=2.0315.
Coefficients derived analytically: a = (3-d)/2 + c·σ*⁴, b = (d-1-4cσ*⁴)/(2σ*²).

### 12v2: TD(lambda) Blending
Instead of blending just NS₂+NS₅, exponentially weight ALL iterates:
w_k = λ^(n-k), normalized. Previous three-point (uniform NS₁+NS₃+NS₅) failed
because NS₁ is too noisy. TD(λ) naturally downweights early iterates.

### 12v3: Adaptive Oscillation Scheduling
Start with stronger oscillation (more exploration), cosine-anneal to weaker
oscillation (more exploitation). Mirrors LR scheduling applied to polynomial dynamics.

## Key Formulas

Bifurcation coefficient derivation (σ*=0.868, c=2.0315):
```
a = (3 - deriv)/2 + 2.0315 * 0.868^4
b = (deriv - 1 - 4*2.0315*0.868^4) / (2*0.868^2)
```

TD(lambda) weights (n=5): w_k = λ^(5-k) / Σλ^(5-j)
- λ=0.3: [0.008, 0.026, 0.086, 0.286, 0.594] (heavy on NS₅)
- λ=0.5: [0.032, 0.065, 0.129, 0.258, 0.516]
- λ=0.7: [0.080, 0.114, 0.163, 0.233, 0.410]
- λ=0.9: [0.152, 0.169, 0.188, 0.209, 0.232] (near-uniform)

## Cost
All three: same as combined mode (matrix path, ~54s at 1.2M). TD(lambda) saves
n-1 extra matrix clones but no extra matmuls.

## Analysis

### Bifurcation Sweep (12v1)
The predicted inverted-U exists but the peak is at weaker oscillation than expected. d=-1.0 (bifurcation boundary, |p'|=1.0) was best, d=-2.8 was catastrophic (+0.007 vs raw Muon). Within combined mode, less polynomial oscillation is better because iterate blending already handles oscillation cancellation. The standard polynomial (d=-1.58) is more oscillatory than optimal when combined with blending. However, d=-1.0's improvement over standard combined was only -0.0013 (within noise).

d=-1.4 anomalously worse than d=-1.58 in same-batch comparison suggests noise at this effect size.

### TD(λ) Blending (12v2) — NEW BEST
λ=0.5 + temporal beat combined IN THE SAME RUN by -0.0044. This is the strongest within-run improvement over combined we've seen.

Why: Combined uses two-point blend (NS₂+NS₅). TD(λ=0.5) weights [0.03, 0.06, 0.13, 0.26, 0.52] across ALL five iterates. NS₃ and NS₄ carry useful oscillation-cancellation information that two-point blending discards. More samples of the oscillatory process → better noise cancellation (Nyquist principle applied to iterate averaging).

Temporal EMA is complementary: λ=0.5 +temporal (-0.016) vs no-temporal (-0.011). Within-step multi-iterate blending and across-step EMA cancel oscillation through orthogonal channels. They stack.

Sweet spot is clearly λ=0.5. λ=0.3 too concentrated on NS₅ (similar to two-point). λ=0.7/0.9 too much early iterate noise.

### Adaptive Scheduling (12v3) — FAILED
Both variants worse than standard combined. If less oscillation is generally better (12a), starting with MORE oscillation is counterproductive even when annealed down. Optimal polynomial dynamics should be constant throughout training.

## Verdict
**TD(λ=0.5) + temporal is the new best: -0.016 vs Muon, -0.004 vs combined (within-run).** Multi-iterate geometric-decay blending is a clean, principled improvement over two-point. Adaptive scheduling is dead. Polynomial tuning (d=-1.0) shows marginal signal but not worth complexity vs TD(λ). One follow-up: combine TD(λ=0.5) + d=-1.0 to see if improvements are additive.
