## Hypothesis

### 12v1: Bifurcation Diagram Sweep
The standard NS polynomial has |p'(σ*)|≈1.58 — just past the period-doubling bifurcation.
Deep research predicts an inverted-U performance curve peaking in the period-2 regime
(|p'| ∈ [1.2, 1.8]). The standard polynomial might not be optimal.

Sweep p'(σ*) from -1.0 to -2.8 while fixing the fixed point and c=2.0315.
Coefficients derived analytically: a = (3-d)/2 + c·σ*⁴, b = (d-1-4cσ*⁴)/(2σ*²).

### 12v2: TD(lambda) Blending
Instead of blending just NS₂+NS₅, exponentially weight ALL iterates:
w_k = λ^(n-k), normalized. Previous three-point (uniform NS₁+NS₃+NS₅) failed
because NS₁ is too noisy. TD(λ) naturally downweights early iterates.

### 12v3: Adaptive Oscillation Scheduling
Start with stronger oscillation (more exploration), cosine-anneal to weaker
oscillation (more exploitation). Mirrors LR scheduling applied to polynomial dynamics.

## Key Formulas

Bifurcation coefficient derivation (σ*=0.868, c=2.0315):
```
a = (3 - deriv)/2 + 2.0315 * 0.868^4
b = (deriv - 1 - 4*2.0315*0.868^4) / (2*0.868^2)
```

TD(lambda) weights (n=5): w_k = λ^(5-k) / Σλ^(5-j)
- λ=0.3: [0.008, 0.026, 0.086, 0.286, 0.594] (heavy on NS₅)
- λ=0.5: [0.032, 0.065, 0.129, 0.258, 0.516]
- λ=0.7: [0.080, 0.114, 0.163, 0.233, 0.410]
- λ=0.9: [0.152, 0.169, 0.188, 0.209, 0.232] (near-uniform)

## Cost
All three: same as combined mode (matrix path, ~54s at 1.2M). TD(lambda) saves
n-1 extra matrix clones but no extra matmuls.
