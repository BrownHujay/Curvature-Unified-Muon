# Experiment 9: CUM v9 — Dampened Late-Stage NS

**File:** `cum/cum_v9.py`
**Status:** PARTIAL SUCCESS

## Hypothesis
Instead of modifying NS OUTPUT (post-hoc blending), modify the NS ITERATION ITSELF. Dampen late-stage steps to preserve curvature within the iteration.

## Architecture
Momentum → Dampened NS (standard steps 1-2, dampened steps 3-5) → Update

## Key Formula
```
For steps k ≥ dampen_after:
X_{k+1} = (1-d) * NS_step(X_k) + d * X_k
```

## Cost
ZERO extra memory. Same matmuls. Just different coefficients.

## Results
| Config | Val Loss | vs Muon | vs v5 |
|--------|----------|---------|-------|
| v9 damp=0.3 | 1.5101 | -0.0089 | +0.0024 |

## Trajectory

|------|------|---------|-------------|

| 1000 | 1.5710 | 1.5634 | 1.5616 (catching v5) |

| Final | 1.5190 | 1.5077 | 1.5101 |

## Analysis
- Beats Muon by 0.0089
- Loses to v5 by 0.0024
- Had the lowest step-1999 loss ever (1.4980) but final eval was higher (1.5101) — possible overfitting
- Dampening is too uniform: slows convergence for ALL SVs equally, whereas v5's snapshot blend naturally captures curvature that matters
- Zero extra memory is appealing but quality gap vs v5 is real

## Verdict
PARTIAL SUCCESS. Proves modifying NS iteration works, but this mechanism is weaker than v5's post-hoc blend.
