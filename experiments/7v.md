# Series 7: Deep Research V2 — Huber SV Mapping

**Status:** ALL FAILED — Monotone mappings cannot match NS

## Hypothesis
Deep Research V2 identified that the optimal SV mapping should:
1. Equalize SVs above a noise threshold (exploit all informative directions)
2. Apply sub-unity contraction (~0.88, not 1.0) for spectral norm regularization
3. Be monotone (unlike the oscillating NS blend)

The proposed Huber mapping f(σ) = min(σ^α, c) combines Schatten-p equalization (via power α) with sub-unity capping (via c=0.88). Two parameters control equalization strength and spectral radius.

## Architecture
```
grad → momentum → SVD → custom SV mapping → reconstruct → update
                   U,S,Vh   f(S/S_max)      U·diag(f)·Vh
```

## Key Formula
- **Huber:** f(σ) = min(σ^α, c)
- **Power:** f(σ) = c · σ^α / max(σ^α)
- **Smooth Huber:** f(σ) = c · tanh(σ^α / c)
- **Scheduled Power:** α cosine-anneals from α_start to α_end over training

Where σ is normalized to [0,1] by dividing by σ_max.

## Cost
Same as 5v6 — one full SVD per step. ~5x slower than NS on GPU (140s vs 30s on H100).

## Analysis

### Why monotone mappings fundamentally fail
NS's polynomial p(σ) = 3.4445σ − 4.775σ³ + 2.0315σ⁵ is NON-MONOTONE. After 5 iterations, it scatters SVs across [0.68, 1.12] in a way that nearly perfectly equalizes them. Any monotone function f(σ) preserving the ordering σ₁ > σ₂ > ... > σₖ must map to f(σ₁) > f(σ₂) > ... > f(σₖ), leaving residual spread. NS's oscillation can "shuffle" SVs, collapsing the spread much more aggressively.

### The α paradox
- α→0 (heavy equalization): maps everything to ~c=0.88 constant. Loses ALL directional info — equivalent to sign(G) which is known to be suboptimal.
- α→1 (no equalization): just scaled SGD. No equalization benefit.
- α=0.1 is "closest" because it barely equalizes — it's almost Muon with extra SVD overhead.
- α=0.3+ equalizes too aggressively via monotone mapping, destroying useful SV structure that NS preserves through its oscillation.

### Cap c doesn't matter much
Varying c from 0.85 to 0.92 at α=0.3 changed val_loss by only 0.007. The equalization strength α dominates; the cap is secondary.

### Deep Research V2 theory was wrong
The hypothesis that a monotone 2-parameter mapping could match NS was incorrect. The key insight: NS's non-monotone oscillation IS the critical feature, not just the sub-unity contraction. The v5/5v6 blend works specifically because it averages oscillating iterates (even/odd anti-correlated), producing a near-constant ~0.88 while preserving the non-monotone mixing that collapses SV spread.

## Verdict
**ALL FAILED.** Confirms that monotone SV mappings cannot match NS's non-monotone oscillation. The only winning strategy remains NS intermediate blending (v5/5v6). 40+ experiments across 7 series confirm nothing else works.
