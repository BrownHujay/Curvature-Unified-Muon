# CUM 5v1: Lie Algebra Momentum + NS

## Status: FAILED (catastrophic)

## Core Idea
Standard Muon does element-wise EMA of gradients — this ignores the matrix structure of weights. The Lie algebra so(m) (skew-symmetric matrices) provides a FLAT space where momentum can be accumulated without vector transport issues.

**Mathematical framework:**
1. Project gradient into Lie algebra: `Ω = 0.5*(g@W.T - W@g.T)` (skew-symmetric)
2. EMA in flat Lie algebra: `Ω̄ = β*Ω̄ + (1-β)*Ω`
3. Nesterov: `Ω_nesterov = Ω + β*Ω̄`
4. Tangent direction: `u = Ω_nesterov @ W` (maps back to m×n)
5. Apply NS for spectral processing (as usual)

## Why This Differs From 3v2 (Cayley, FAILED)
- 3v2 computed `A@W` from a SINGLE step's gradient — no persistent state
- 5v1 accumulates `Ω̄` across steps via EMA in the Lie algebra
- The Lie algebra EMA encodes "which rotation has been consistently useful"
- NS still handles spectral processing (we're augmenting, not replacing)

## Cost
- ~20% more than Muon (3 extra matmuls per step for Lie algebra projection)
- Memory: one m×m (or n×n) skew-symmetric buffer per weight

## Source
Inspired by: Lie group methods in differential equations, Riemannian optimization on SO(n)

## Results
- **val_loss = 3.2463** (+1.73 vs Muon 1.5169) — catastrophic failure

## Analysis
The Lie algebra projection `Ω = 0.5*(g@W.T - W@g.T)` keeps only the **antisymmetric** (rotational) part and discards the **symmetric** (scaling/stretching) part. The symmetric part carries most of the optimization signal. Additionally, `u = Ω@W` is quadratic in W's scale, making the direction magnitude-dependent on weight norms rather than gradient information. NS can't fix a fundamentally wrong direction.
