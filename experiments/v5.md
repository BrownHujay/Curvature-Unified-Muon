# Experiment 5: CUM v5 — Multi-Resolution NS ⭐

**File:** `cum/cum_v5.py`
**Status:** BEST RESULT

## Hypothesis
Instead of blending NS(5) with the raw gradient (noisy), blend with a partially-converged NS intermediate (denoised). Save NS state at step k — it's been partially denoised but retains curvature info.

## Key Insight
NS iteration progressively equalizes SVs. At step 2, ~25% of original SV spread remains. At step 3, ~5% remains. Step-2 intermediate is partially denoised while retaining significant curvature structure.

## Architecture
Momentum → NS₅(u) with save at step k → Blend NS₅ with NS_k → Update

## Key Formula
```
update = (1-α) * NS₅(u) + α * scale_match(NS₂(u))
```

## Cost
One `.clone()` at save point. No extra matmuls.

## Analysis
- save@2 >> save@3: Step-2 intermediate has much more curvature info
- b=0.15 slightly better than b=0.1: more blend from denoised source helps
- 2.6x bigger improvement than v3's raw gradient blend (0.0113 vs 0.0044)
- v5 starts slightly slower but accelerates in second half — curvature info more valuable as training progresses

## Why v5 > v3
- v3: NS₅ + raw normalized gradient = maximum curvature, maximum noise
- v5: NS₅ + NS₂ = significant curvature, reduced noise
- NS₂ has had 2 steps of SV compression, removing noisiest components while preserving dominant curvature

## Verdict
BEST RESULT. Multi-resolution NS is the clear winner. b=0.15 confirmed optimal for save@2.
