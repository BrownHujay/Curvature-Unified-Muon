## Analysis

### What stacks (estimated individual contributions)
1. **Per-head QKV 4 slices:** ~-0.019 (replicated, robust)
2. **Weaker polynomial (d=-1.0) + TD(λ=0.5):** ~-0.010 additional
3. **Out_proj column slicing (4 slices):** ~-0.005 additional
4. **MLP slicing:** NEGATIVE. Don't do it.

### Why combined mode fails on per-head
Per-head slices (96×128) are much closer to square than full QKV (384×128). NS converges cleanly on near-square matrices — less inter-iterate oscillation to cancel. Two-point iterate blend was fixing a problem that per-head already solved. Temporal EMA becomes pure lag. But the weaker polynomial (d=-1.0) changes the convergence *target*, not just oscillation management, so it still helps.

### 16f: 124M Scale Test (GPT-2 Small / OpenWebText, 2000 steps batch 32)

Second attempt (3 and 4 slices, 2k steps batch 32):
| Config | Val Loss | vs Muon | Time |
|--------|----------|---------|------|
| Muon+AdamW | 4.8355 | — | 208s |
| PerHead 4s td | 4.8291 | -0.0064 | 255s |
| PerHead 3s td (Q/K/V) | 4.8363 | +0.0008 | 250s |

**CRITICAL FINDING: Per-head does NOT scale to 124M.** The improvement is aspect-ratio dependent:
- Per-head slice ratio = 3/n_heads
- 4 heads (1.2M): 3/4 = 0.75:1 (near square, NS thrives) → -0.025
- 12 heads (124M): 3/12 = 0.25:1 (very rectangular) → ~0
- Fewer slices at 124M (3s, 4s): barely helps (-0.006 at best)

The per-head effect at 1.2M was driven by the lucky coincidence that 4 heads with d_model=128 produces near-square NS slices. It's not a fundamental improvement — it's geometry-dependent.

## Revised Analysis

### Confirmed contributions at 1.2M
1. **Per-head QKV plain:** -0.019 ± 0.003 (6 runs)
2. **TD(λ=0.5) + d=-1.0 on per-head:** -0.025 ± 0.002 (4 runs)
3. **Out_proj col-slicing:** noise (confirmed in 16e)

### At 124M
- Per-head: -0.006 at best (doesn't scale)
- Blending recipe alone (Series 11c): -0.014 (scales better)

## Verdict
**Per-head orthogonalization is a small-scale phenomenon.** Real and robust at 1.2M, but driven by aspect ratio rather than fundamental spectral separation. Does not transfer to 124M where more heads create worse slice ratios. The blending recipe (TD + temporal + d=-1.0 on full QKV) remains the best approach that scales. NS optimization is fully exhausted after 16 series and 85+ experiments.
