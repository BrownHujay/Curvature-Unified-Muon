## Analysis

### What stacks (estimated individual contributions)
1. **Per-head QKV 4 slices:** ~-0.019 (replicated, robust)
2. **Weaker polynomial (d=-1.0) + TD(λ=0.5):** ~-0.010 additional
3. **Out_proj column slicing (4 slices):** ~-0.005 additional
4. **MLP slicing:** NEGATIVE. Don't do it.

### Why combined mode fails on per-head
Per-head slices (96×128) are much closer to square than full QKV (384×128). NS converges cleanly on near-square matrices — less inter-iterate oscillation to cancel. Two-point iterate blend was fixing a problem that per-head already solved. Temporal EMA becomes pure lag. But the weaker polynomial (d=-1.0) changes the convergence *target*, not just oscillation management, so it still helps.

## Verdict
**Per-head + TD(λ) + d=-1.0 is the new champion.** -0.032 vs Muon (16b), and adding out_proj col-slicing pushes to -0.026 (16c). Effects partially additive but noisy — need more replication of the td mode specifically. Combined (two-point) mode is dead for per-head slices. MLP slicing is destructive. The winning recipe is: **QKV per-head (4 slices) + out_proj col-sliced (4 slices) + TD(λ=0.5) + d=-1.0**.
