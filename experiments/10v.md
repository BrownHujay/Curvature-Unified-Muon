## Analysis

### Extended NS two-point blends are noise
- Run 1: both beat Muon by ~0.002 → Run 2: both lose to Muon by ~0.005
- The extended blends themselves are consistent (1.520 both runs), but Muon fluctuates ±0.006
- **Conclusion: the "s3+NS7 beats Muon" finding from 8a was noise.** Both extended blends are Muon-tier at best.

### Early convergence is real but fades
- s3+NS7 consistently fastest at step 250 (2.338 this run, 2.339 last run)
- But by step 1000 Muon catches up, and by step 1500 Muon passes them
- More NS steps → more oscillation points but also more accumulated polynomial error

### Cross-run noise is ±0.006
- Muon swings 1.515-1.522, 5v6 swings 1.507-1.518
- Within-run deltas are reliable; cross-run comparisons need ≥3 runs to be confident
- torch.compile nondeterminism is the main source

### 5v6 is the real deal
- Beats Muon by 0.004-0.008 across both runs
- SVD path's exact SV computation gives it consistent edge

## Verdict
**FAILED.** Extended NS two-point blends (NS₂+NS₈, NS₃+NS₇) are not reliably better than Muon NS=5. Previous 8a result was noise. The ±0.006 cross-run variance means only improvements >0.008 are trustworthy.
