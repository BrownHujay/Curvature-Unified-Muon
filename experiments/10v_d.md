## Analysis

### Combined: Double oscillation cancellation
The combined mode does two layers of oscillation cancellation:
1. **Within-step (iterate blend):** NS₂ + NS₅ from the SAME gradient → cancels iteration-axis oscillation instantly
2. **Across-step (temporal blend):** EMA of blended outputs → cancels temporal-axis oscillation

Input-blend only has layer 2. When gradients shift late in training (loss flattening), the EMA becomes stale. Combined's layer 1 provides INSTANTANEOUS cancellation from the current gradient, making it robust to distribution shift. The EMA handles residual oscillation.

This explains the trajectory: combined and input-blend are similar early (both benefit from temporal smoothing), but combined PULLS AWAY late (layer 1 remains effective as layer 2 weakens).

### Input-blend instability revealed
| Run | input-blend vs Muon |

| Run 1 (9a) | -0.0111 |

Input-blend can have bad runs. Pure temporal averaging is sensitive to the specific sequence of gradients encountered. This weakens the "confirmed Pareto-optimal" claim from 10c.

### Scheduled three-point: modest improvement
- 1.5173 (-0.003 vs Muon) — better than unscheduled three-point (+0.002 to +0.011)
- Annealing NS₁ weight helps but the improvement is small and likely in noise range
- Not competitive with combined or input-blend

### Combined's late-game acceleration is suspicious (in a good way)
- Most variants plateau or worsen after step 1500
- Combined keeps improving: 1.495 → 1.491 → 1.504
- The gap vs Muon GROWS: -0.021 at 1500, -0.025 at 1750, -0.021 at 1999
- Systematic divergence is harder to attribute to noise than a lucky final value

### Reliability caveat
- Only ONE run of combined. Need replication.
- Input-blend's instability in this same run shows single runs can be misleading
- The systematic trajectory is encouraging but not proof

## Verdict
**Combined: EXTREMELY PROMISING (-0.019 vs Muon at matrix speed) but needs replication.** If real, this is 2x the improvement of input-blend/5v6, at 4x the speed of 5v6. The double oscillation cancellation theory is compelling.

**Scheduled three-point: MARGINAL.** -0.003 vs Muon, not worth the complexity.
