# Experiment 10: CUM v10 — Dampened NS + Multi-Resolution Blend

**File:** `cum/cum_v10.py`
**Status:** FAILED

## Hypothesis
Combine v5 (multi-resolution blend) and v9 (dampened iteration). Both independently beat Muon, so combining should be better.

## Architecture
Momentum → Dampened NS with intermediate save → Blend dampened output with intermediate → Update

## Cost
Extra tensor allocations in inner NS loop (the killer).

## Results
| Config | Val Loss (step 500) | Time (500 steps) |
|--------|---------------------|-------------------|
| v10 damp+blend | 1.8391 | 529s |
| Muon (reference) | 1.7830 | 145s |

Killed early — far behind AND 3.6x slower.

## Analysis
- Inner-loop interpolation `(1-d)*X_next + d*X` breaks in-place tensor optimization
- Creates extra tensor allocations every NS step → 3.6x slowdown
- Double curvature preservation is TOO MUCH — signal becomes noise-dominated
- Two curvature-recovery mechanisms interfere rather than compose

## Verdict
FAILED. Combining two curvature-recovery mechanisms is worse than either alone.
