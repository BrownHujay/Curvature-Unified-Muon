## Hypothesis
The only proven strategy (40+ experiments) is NS intermediate iterate blending (v5/5v6). We systematically explore the blending space:
1. Different blend pairs beyond NS₂+NS₅ (e.g. NS₂+NS₈, NS₃+NS₇)
2. Extended NS past 5 steps (7, 8) — more oscillation to cancel
3. Three-point blends (NS₁+NS₃+NS₅) — more interpolation points
4. SV-space geometric blending — does the blend operation matter?
5. Temporal input blending — EMA of past NS outputs

## Architecture
```
grad → momentum → [matrix NS / SVD] → blend iterates → update
                    ↓
            save intermediates at specified steps
            or compute SV mappings at specified iteration counts
```

6. Combined iterate + input blend — within-step AND across-step cancellation
7. Adaptive residual — corrections weighted by their relative magnitude
8. Cosine-gated — corrections only applied when curvature & temporal signals agree
9. Scheduled three-point — NS₁ weight anneals from 0.05→0 over training

## Key Formulas
- **Two-point:** `(1-b) * NS_n + b * scale(NS_k)`
- **Three-point:** `(1-a-b) * NS_n + b * scale(NS_j) + a * scale(NS_i)`
- **Geometric SV:** `S_out = NS_n(σ)^(1-b) * NS_k(σ)^b`
- **Input blend:** `(1-α) * NS₅(u_t) + α * scale(EMA(NS₅(u_{<t})))`
- **Combined:** iterate_blend(NS₂, NS₅) then input_blend(result, EMA)
- **Adaptive residual:** `NS₅ + α(‖δ‖)*δ_curv + β(‖δ‖)*δ_temp` (trust proportional to magnitude)
- **Cosine-gated:** `NS₅ + gate*(α*δ_curv + β*δ_temp)` where gate = max(0, cos(δ_curv, δ_temp))
- **Scheduled three:** three-point with NS₁ weight cosine-annealed to 0

## Cost
- Matrix two/three-point: Same as Muon (saves intermediates, no extra matmuls)
- SVD modes: ~5x slower (full SVD per step)
- Input blend: One extra matrix in state (EMA buffer)

## Results

### 8a: Two-Point Blend Exploration (A100 GPU, batch=32, 2000 steps)
| Config | Val Loss | vs Muon | vs 5v6 | Time |
|--------|----------|---------|--------|------|
| 5v6 s2 b=0.25 | 1.5178 | -0.0039 | — | 213s |
| 8v1 s3+NS7 b=0.15 | 1.5193 | -0.0025 | +0.0014 | 57s |
| 8v1 s2+NS8 b=0.15 | 1.5203 | -0.0015 | +0.0025 | 61s |
| Muon NS=5 | 1.5218 | — | +0.0039 | 46s |

**Finding:** Save point matters more than total steps. s3+NS7 > s2+NS8. Both beat Muon but not 5v6. Matrix path is 3.5x faster.

### 8b: Three-Point + SV-Space (A100 GPU, batch=32, 2000 steps)
| Config | Val Loss | vs Muon | vs 5v6 | Time |
|--------|----------|---------|--------|------|
| 5v6 s2 b=0.25 | 1.5076 | -0.0043 | — | 213s |
| 8v1 geom s2 b=0.15 | 1.5087 | -0.0032 | +0.0011 | 215s |
| Muon NS=5 | 1.5119 | — | +0.0043 | 39s |
| 8v1 3pt 1+3+5 | 1.5225 | +0.0106 | +0.0149 | 53s |

**Finding:** Geometric SV blend ties 5v6 (same SVD cost). Three-point WORSE than Muon — NS₁ too noisy late. BUT three-point had fastest early convergence ever (2.394 at step 250). NS₁ helps early, hurts late.
