## Analysis

### "Intelligent" weighting adds nothing
- Adaptive residual: -0.002 vs Muon (noise)
- Cosine-gated: ±0.000 vs Muon (dead tie)
- Both are 50% SLOWER than Muon (60s vs 40s) for zero benefit
- The curvature and temporal corrections ARE detected (non-zero residuals) but weighting them by magnitude or agreement doesn't help

### Why smart weighting fails: oscillation cancellation is statistical
The NS polynomial oscillation produces structured noise across ALL singular values. Cancellation works by averaging many small corrections — it's a Central Limit Theorem effect. When you gate or weight individual corrections:
- **Magnitude weighting** (adaptive-res): amplifies large corrections, which may be outliers, and suppresses small ones, which collectively carry the signal
- **Agreement gating** (cosine-gated): curvature (NS₂-NS₅) and temporal (EMA-NS₅) corrections point in different directions for good reason — they capture different oscillation axes. Requiring agreement throws away complementary information.

### Simple beats smart
- Input-blend (simple EMA + blend): mean -0.005 vs Muon
- Combined (simple iterate blend + simple EMA): -0.019 vs Muon (1 run)
- Adaptive residual (magnitude-weighted): -0.002 vs Muon
- Cosine-gated (agreement-filtered): ±0.000 vs Muon

The pattern: stacking SIMPLE averaging operations works. Adding COMPLEX weighting logic doesn't. The "intelligence" breaks the statistical cancellation.

### Input-blend is real but noisy
- Mean delta: -0.005 vs Muon with std 0.006
- Two great runs (-0.010, -0.011), one bad (+0.004), one moderate (-0.004)
- Still positive in expectation but not reliably strong in any single run

## Key Learnings
- **36. Smart correction weighting fails.** Magnitude-based and agreement-based gating both produce Muon-tier results. The corrections exist but intelligent weighting breaks statistical cancellation.
- **37. Simple averaging beats complex weighting.** Combined's power comes from two layers of simple blend, not from clever per-element logic.
- **38. Input-blend mean improvement is -0.005±0.006 vs Muon.** Real but noisy. Not the -0.010 initially claimed.

## Verdict
**Adaptive residual: FAILED.** Muon-tier, 50% slower.
**Cosine-gated: FAILED.** Dead tie with Muon, 50% slower.
**Combined remains the most promising approach** — simple double cancellation, not smart single cancellation.
